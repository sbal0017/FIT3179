Terrorism around the World

A journey through the worlds Terrorist Attacks and Incidents



285,098 attacks have been carried out worldwide from a total of 166 different countries. This makes Terrorism one of the largest dangers to society and has been a prominent issue for a very long time.

The following dataset looks into the history of terrorism, the attacks they carry out, groups that exist and the people they effect, trying to draw specific trends between the events.


The following visualisations are based on all data drawn from the GTD (Global Terrorism Database) and ranges from 2000, all the way until 2015.

Terrorist Attacks around the World

From 2000 - 2015

Terrorism is a problem all around the world. To the right, you can see the count of Terrorism
based attacks per country and how certain regions are far worse affected than others.

The data is not normalised as Terrorism isn't based on a population, rather
a will to provoke and attack certain groups of people, inciting fear.

Attacks and Deaths per Year

A Total of 285,098 Attacks and 210,148 Deaths

Many different groups pull off a variety of attacks each year. Every single attack is different and devestating in one way or another.
To the left, we can see the gradual trend throughout the years of an increasing count of both Attacks and Deaths from Terrorism

The deaths data includes casualties and if relevent, the terrorist(s) themselves.

Specific Incident Locations From Iraq

The country with the largest count of Deaths and Attacks

Iraq is a hotspot of Terrorism, housing a large number of International Terrorist Groups. It had 18,649 Attacks and 59,228 Deaths ranging from 2000 - 2015.
Each attack uses a different type of method to increase its potential threat. To the right, the specific incidents and attack types are plotted on the map.

Select a Specific Attack Type to filter out Data.

Weapon Types used in Terrorist Attacks

Based on the Top 10 most Dangerous Countries

There are a large range of Weapons used in attacks, each with its own devestating effects. To the left is a map of the preferred weapon choice based on 10 of the highest attack count countries.
Explosives are by far the most dangerous and common weapon type, making up a total of 49,811 choices compared to the 87,366 in total.

Not every Terrorist incident had a weapon type included (some are unknown or other), hence having less data than the total attacks.
